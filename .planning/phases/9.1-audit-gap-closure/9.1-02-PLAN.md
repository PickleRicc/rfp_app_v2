DRAFT-06 requires: "System identifies required fill-in templates and maps customer data to correct template fields."

**What IS populated from customer data:**
- Staffing plans: key_personnel names, roles, and qualifications from Tier 2 data call
- Past performance forms: contract references, agency names, contract values, relevance from Tier 2 data call

**What uses [PLACEHOLDER] markers:**
- Cost/price volume: ALL dollar amounts, hourly rates, FTE counts, and percentages use `[PLACEHOLDER: $X]` markers
- The user fills these in when completing the pricing spreadsheet in Microsoft Word
- The narrative structure, CLIN references, contract type, and pricing methodology ARE populated — only actual numbers are placeholders

**Why this design was chosen:**
- CONTEXT.md delegated "Template population approach for fill-in forms" to Claude's discretion
- Government cost proposals require exact pricing that cannot be AI-generated — rates come from approved rate tables, GSA schedules, or internal pricing models
- Generating fake dollar amounts would be worse than placeholders — users would need to verify and replace every number anyway
- The system DOES identify the template structure and map the narrative framework correctly

**The question:**
Is it acceptable that cost/price dollar amounts use [PLACEHOLDER] markers while the narrative framework, CLIN structure, and contract context are populated? Or should the system attempt to inject Tier 2 data (which currently has no cost/price fields) into the dollar amount positions?
